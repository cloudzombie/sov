# Mining-tax accounts — secure generation plan

> **Interactive companion:** open [`genesis.html`](genesis.html) in a browser for a
> step-by-step ceremony checklist that validates your public keys locally (and
> refuses anything that looks like a seed), generates the exact lines to send, and
> saves your progress as you confirm each step.

SOV pays a **perpetual tax on every block**: of each coinbase subsidy *and* each
transaction fee, **5% → a founder account, 2% → a dev account, 93% → the miner**
(no burn). The two tax accounts are `tax_primary_recipient` /
`tax_secondary_recipient` in `MiningPolicy` (consensus constants); today they are
placeholders. This plan generates the **real** accounts you control, validates the
tax flowing into them on testnet-1, and prepares the identical binding for
mainnet.

## Non-negotiable security principle

**The seeds are generated by you and never touch this assistant, this repo, or
the network.** Key generation happens on *your* machine. You hand over only the
**public key** of each account — a value that is, by design, safe to publish on
chain. Whoever holds a mnemonic owns that account's tax stream forever, so treat
the mnemonics like the crown jewels.

The tax accounts **must be key-bound in genesis (block 0)**. A tax account left
keyless would be claimable by the first `RotateKey` to reach it — i.e. the very
first block's tax could be stolen. Binding your public keys at genesis closes
that window before a single coin is minted.

---

## Step 1 — generate the two accounts (you, privately)

Build the wallet once (`cd chain && cargo build --release`), then generate **two
separate accounts with two independent seeds**:

```sh
./target/release/sov-wallet new founder.tax.sov     # the 5% account
./target/release/sov-wallet new dev.tax.sov         # the 2% account
```

Each run prints, **for your eyes only**, a fresh 24-word BIP-39 mnemonic and the
account's keys. It is fully offline — nothing is sent anywhere.

```
================= SECRET — WRITE THIS DOWN, NEVER SHARE =================
mnemonic (24 words) — the ONLY backup; whoever holds it owns the account:
  <24 words>
========================================================================

PUBLIC — safe to share; this is what gets registered/hardcoded on-chain:
path       : m/44'/5459798'/0'/0'/0'
scheme     : hybrid65
public_key : hybrid65:0x….               <-- give me ONLY this line
shielded   : xus1….
```

For each account: **record the mnemonic securely** (write it on paper / store in a
password manager or hardware-backed vault) and keep the two separate. The keys are
**hybrid Ed25519 + ML-DSA-65** (post-quantum) by default — the right choice for a
balance that compounds for years.

> **For the mainnet-grade ceremony** (if these are your forever accounts): run the
> generation on an **air-gapped / freshly-booted** machine, store each mnemonic in
> a separate safe, and never type either mnemonic into an internet-connected host
> except to sign. You can confirm recovery any time with
> `sov-wallet import "<mnemonic>"` — it must reprint the same `public_key`.

## Step 2 — send me only the public keys

Paste back **just these two lines** (the `public_key:` value from each run), and
confirm the account names:

```
founder (5%): hybrid65:0x…
dev     (2%): hybrid65:0x…
```

That is all I need. **Never paste a mnemonic, a seed, or a `seed_hex`.** If you
ever do by accident, discard those accounts and regenerate — a leaked seed is a
lost account.

## Step 3 — I bind them into the chain (no seeds involved)

With your two public keys I will:

1. Add both as **key-bound, zero-balance genesis accounts** to
   `chain/specs/testnet-1.json` (`founder.tax.sov` → your key 1, `dev.tax.sov` →
   your key 2). Zero balance = still no pre-mine; the keys just establish control.
2. Re-pin the testnet-1 **genesis hash** (it changes when genesis accounts change)
   and update the determinism KAT to the new value.
3. `sov-testnet status` already prints both tax balances (see below).

(`MiningPolicy` already names `founder.tax.sov` / `dev.tax.sov`, so no Rust change
is needed unless you want different account names — tell me and I'll rename them
across the policy, tests, and KAT vectors.)

## Step 4 — relaunch and watch the tax arrive

Relaunch testnet-1 (`sov-testnet join … && up`). Every 60-second block now pays
5% / 2% of its 12.5-XUS coinbase to your accounts:

```sh
./target/release/sov-testnet status --out testnet1
# mining tax (5% / 2% of every coinbase + fee):
#   founder 5%  founder.tax.sov   <climbing> XUS
#   dev     2%  dev.tax.sov       <climbing> XUS
```

After ~N blocks the balances must be exactly `N × 0.625 XUS` (founder) and
`N × 0.25 XUS` (dev), plus the 5:2 split of any transaction fees.

## Step 5 — prove you control them (the real validation)

Seeing a balance is not control. Confirm you can **spend** the tax with your
mnemonic — the node accepts the transfer only if the signature matches the key
bound in genesis:

```sh
./target/release/sov-wallet 127.0.0.1:8645 transfer \
  "<founder mnemonic>" founder.tax.sov <some-test-account> 1
```

(or `sov-wallet import "<mnemonic>"` to re-derive, then transfer.) A successful,
node-accepted spend from `founder.tax.sov` is the end-to-end proof that the tax is
landing in an account **you, and only you, control**.

## Step 6 — mainnet

When testnet-1 has validated the wallet, the **same** two public keys are bound
into the frozen `sov-mainnet` genesis the same way (or you regenerate dedicated
mainnet accounts with the Step-1 air-gapped ceremony — your call). From that point
the founder/dev tax is yours, forever, on every block ever mined.
